Introducing openSUSE Reach

written by Nycticorax on 2020-12-30

openSUSE Reach

"openSUSE Reach" conceptualizes a web application meant to strengthen the openSUSE community along four dimensions

  1. facilitating knowledge transfer across the community
  2. facilitating recruitment of new users, contributors and maintainers
  3. helping openSUSE sub-projects strive
  4. creating stronger community bonds

Rules

  1. A sub-project is defined, specifying the following properties:
    • a start and optionally, an end date
    • a description of the goal and first milestone
    • a specification of the required skills, on the scale <"welcoming greenhorns", "experienced", "veterans">
    • a min. and optionally, a max. number of participants
  2. Whenever a user joins the community they are offered the option to opt out from oS Reach. If they don't opt out, they must fill a form asking for their skills across different preexisting categories, as well as the max. of projects they agree to concurrently work on.
  3. At any point a user can express their interest to a sub-project my subscribing to it. This means they will receive email notifications whenever os Reach thinks they would be a good fit for a position in the project. Whenever a user is sent a notification inviting them to contribute to a project, they can accept it. Accepting an invite materializes a commitment between users and sub-projects.
  4. Whenever oS Reach detects a change in the system, it sees whether it can act on it for the purpose of matching members and projects. oS Reach tries to fill all opened positions across all sub-projects, closing positions whenever a user accepts an invite to fill it, keeping track of all positions and sending invite notifications. In doing so oS Reach prioritizes subscribed users over non-subscribed users, but explores matches further into non-subscribed users territory when subscribed users are not enough.
  5. When all positions in a sub-project are occupied, the project maintainer is notified appropriately. The project maintainer can then agree to close the gates for the project (a project maintainer can close the gates at any time). If the gates are closed, all participants are notified that work is starting, and the project can no longer be subscribed to. From then on the project maintainer is responsible for updating oS Reach on all things regarding the sub-project.
